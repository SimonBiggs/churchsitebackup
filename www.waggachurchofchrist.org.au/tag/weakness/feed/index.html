]]> I was listening to a documentary on the Vikings earlier today. They were talking about the inability of the Vikings to understand a god who would passively lay down His life. The Vikings believed in warrior gods. So to have a powerful god not fight was beyond understanding for them. They wrote him off as a weakling.

When you think about it the notion does seem ridiculous. Why would God who is all powerful simply surrender His own son to die at the hands of a rebellious sinful and very weak people? The answer comes partly in the form of grace and love. It is the message of the famous verse of John 3:16, ‘For God loved the world so much that He gave is only Son that whoeverÂ believes in Him should not perish but have eternal life.’

On the other hand, there is an answer for the Viking too. You can not really kill God’s Son. He simply rose again from the dead and displayed the power of God. He will come again next time in all of His power too.

Jesus humbled himself at the Father’s will to raise up a great people. Not a weakened people of slavery but a great family of God. The Vikings liked victories. Jesus won the ultimate Victory. Victory over death. It’s just that He had to die first before He could destroy it. As Scripture says…’Whoever has the Son has life, whoever does not have the Son of God does not have life” 1 John 5:12

Pastor Ross Brinkman
