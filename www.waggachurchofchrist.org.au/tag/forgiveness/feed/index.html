]]> Col 3:13 says ‘Forgive as the Lord forgave you.’

Forgiveness is not an option for a Christian. It is both a command and an example already set for us to follow.

In the Lords prayer He taught that we should ask forgiveness as we have forgiven others. 
On the cross he asked forgiveness for his executioners. He went to the Cross in obedience to His father 
who sent him there that we could be forgiven. So Christians, if they will really follow Christ, will learn to forgive as He did.
I was listening to a breakfast news program this week when the reporters spoke in amazement about a man who was at church when the young man that had 
kill his son with a vehicle while under the influence of drugs arrived at church. This Christian man went up and hugged the young man.
This act of forgiveness, the reporters acknowledged, was amazing. Actually the Christian father spoke very honestly when he admitted that he had not fully achieved forgiveness yet.
His Christian commitment however is obvious because forgiveness was the direction he was deliberately heading. 

If you wish to follow Christ then forgiveness must also be a characteristic of your life. No exceptions. Trust is earned but your forgiveness must not have to be earned. 
Forgiveness is in this way is like love for the bible declares that we love because He first loved us
We also forgive because He first forgave us. Even if the other person is unrepentant we must forgive. Forgiveness and unforgiveness are both alike in this one regard.
Both will change who we are. One will change you to be like God in character, the other will make you unlike Him.
