Shaw Family (Perry and Karen)
Baertschi Family (Thomas and Clare)

The Making of a Home!

We are slowly and surely making our home here. It’s incredible the little things that make a difference. When we were unpacking, I was amazed that for me (Clare), it was my coffee cups. To finally have them unpacked and open the cupboard to have a coffee, and there's MY mug. My old, slightly stained, and faded mug. That was "home".  And just like that mug, we’re learning (again) here, that we don’t need to be perfect. The Lord can take us, slightly stained and faded, and use us in His ways and His timing.

Visas and entry problems!

Back in May, the Australian government put a hold in 457 visas. These are the exact visas that MAF staff use to work here. While this didn’t affect Thomas’ job directly, many of the people who were on Orientation with us were suddenly told that they had to go home, or go to another program. For us, this was another wave of grief – that new friends we’d been excited to start this adventure with, were not going to be turning up. For the families affected, there was confusion and uncertainty. We all had to pray and trust that the Lord would work it all out in His ways and His timing. Three months later, we were rejoicing as the Australian government placed pilots and avionics engineers back on the “eligible” list! This change has been HUGE for the team here! It now means that we get 4 more pilots and 1 engineer! They will be an AMAZING blessing to the program here.

Church

We have been attending the Australian Aboriginal Outreach Mission Church. We had a choice of 3 churches here, but felt called to this one, as half the congregation is Yolŋu. Due to Thomas’ work in the hangar, he doesn’t encounter the Yolŋu people as much as pilots do in their work, so we need to be deliberate about forming friendships and getting to know the people here.
We worship in a tin shed, with roller doors on each side that get opened as the humidity drops and the breeze increases.  The service is led by a team of Yolŋu men, who sing with great passion and wonderful harmonies. The songs are the same each week, which offers the opportunity to learn some Yolŋu Matha – one of the main languages here.

For Those Who Pray…

Praise that we are feeling at home here in this new place!
Praise that the 457 visa now includes pilots & avionics engineers! – Pray that they will be granted for those who have applied.

Operation Christmas Child (Samaritan's Purse)

Each year, Wagga Church of Christ participates in Operation Christmas Child a global ministry initiative of Samaritan's Purse.

We pack shoeboxes full of gifts for children in other countries across the world.

This is an amazing ministry of giving that brings hope and a smile to so many children much less fortunate than us.

Bec and Joy packing the shoeboxes - this ministry brings our whole church together on one exciting packing day each year!
